 5  """ 
 6  Logging of information from the pyMetabolism package is organised in a 
 7  hierarchical fashion. pyMetabolism uses L{logging} for this pupose. If you want 
 8  to use the logging facilities for your own purposes simply create your own top- 
 9  level logger called "pyMetabolism" and then attach a handler of your choosing to 
10  it. All package internal loggers inherit from "pyMetabolism" so make sure not to 
11  over-ride your logger instance's attribute perpetuate to C{False}. A simple code 
12  example of using logging with pyMetabolism is below. 
13   
14  >>> import logging 
15  >>> logger = logging.getLogger("pyMetabolism") 
16  >>> handler = logging.StreamHandler() 
17  >>> handler.setLevel(logging.DEBUG) 
18  >>> logger.addHandler(handler) 
19   
20  Now all messages from within the pyMetabolism modules are propagated up to this 
21  logger and then handled by your handler. If you want more fine-grained control 
22  you can access the sub-loggers and attach other handlers to them. The logger 
23  inheritance path is always as follows: 
24   
25  pyMetabolism.Class.instance 
26   
27  That means if you want all messages occurring in reactions sent to you by e-mail 
28  because they're so pretty then you can get the logger easily: 
29   
30  >>> reaction_logger = logging.getLogger("pyMetabolism.Reaction") 
31   
32  Just add your preferred handler to this logger and you're good to go. 
33
